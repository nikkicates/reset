Introduction
What Is Actually Happening
5 min read

You were promised that AI would free you. For a lot of us, that is not what happened.

Instead, the tools arrived and the work multiplied. The device is always present, which means you are always reachable, which means work never fully stops. There is a name for this: Ambient Work. It bleeds into your evenings and weekends not because you are bad at boundaries, but because the system was designed without them.

On top of that, many of us have become the unofficial quality control layer for everyone else's half-finished automation. You verify. You translate. You clean it up. That is called Workslop, and the cognitive drain of managing it has a name too: #AIBrainFry.

Three Terms Worth Knowing

Ambient Work — Work that bleeds into your off-hours because the device and the expectation of availability never stop.

Workslop — Low-quality machine-generated output that lands on your desk to be fixed. You did not create the mess. You are expected to clean it up.

#AIBrainFry — The chronic cognitive exhaustion of verifying, translating, and masking machine output on top of your actual responsibilities.

For BIWOC navigating professional environments, there is an additional layer. There is a cost to editing your voice, your presence, your way of communicating so it lands safely in spaces that were not built for you. That cost is real, measurable, and it accumulates.

Structural Sovereignty is what happens when you stop absorbing a broken structure and start designing your professional life around your actual capacity, your actual authority, and your actual voice. That is what this intensive is for.
